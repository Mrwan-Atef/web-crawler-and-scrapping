at the Phoenix Mountain Preserve to exploring museums on a budget here are Phoenix s best free experiences Caitlin O Hara Lonely Planet The sprawling city of Phoenix Arizona may have a pricey metropolitan feel downtown but it also hosts a plethora of free or cheap outdoor arts adventure and cultural activities Scattered across the Valley of the Sun dozens of museums trails markets and historic sites exist to visit at low cost â or for nothing at all Whether youâ re a regular or first time visitor here s how to see the best of Phoenix and the surrounding area for free Experience Phoenix s contrasts at Heritage Square Heritage Square is a cluster of late Victorian and early century homes that stand in stark contrast to the soaring modernity of downtown Phoenix In fact this city block is actually on the National Register of Historic Places With the buildings now sensitively repurposed it s possible to see inside many Planning tip It s not all free though If you wish to enter the stately and well restored Rosson House Museum expect to pay a admission fee Learn about the state at Arizona Capitol Museum The Arizona Capitol Museum serves as a symbol of the state s vast and colorful history Guests can experience a range of educational opportunities â for free â that offer visibility into governmental political social and cultural chronicles of the state Advertisement Visit the Phoenix Art Museum and see a dinosaur in a red cage Alamy Stock Photo Time your visit to Phoenix Art Museum just right The Phoenix Art Museum is Arizona s premier repository of fine art including works by Claude Monet Diego Rivera and Georgia O Keeffe On Pay What You Wish Wednesday entry is by voluntary donation after Look out for AfterHours free evening sessions at the museum with live music and entertainment â there are four a year Family Fundays â also four times a year â happen on Sundays with free access and activities aimed at art enthusiasts of all ages Planning tip Make a beeline for the Western Gallery to see how the astonishing Arizona landscape has inspired everyone from the early pioneers to modernists Try the trails at Camelback Mountain BCFC Shutterstock Hike the challenging trails at Camelback Mountain Camelback Mountain is considered one of the nation s top hiking destinations This ft twin humped mountain sits smack in the center of all the Phoenix action The two main trails are rated extremely difficult and subject hikers to elevation gains uneven terrain and a path that is unprotected from the elements so make sure you bring plenty of water and take adequate rests on the way to the top The Echo Canyon Trail E McDonald Dr is short but steep with of elevation gain over a mere miles each way and lots of hands on scrambling over boulders It s a great workout followed by stellar views of the Valley of the Sun Heard Museum is free to visit on the first Friday of most months The impressive historic Heard Museum spotlights the culture life and arts of American Indian tribes in the Southwest Visitors will find art galleries ethnographic displays films a get creative kids exhibit and an unrivaled collection of Hopi kachinas elaborate spirit dolls many gifted by Presidential nominee Barry Goldwater Planning tip On the first Friday of every month except March it s free to visit the Heard Museum from Keep a lookout for occasional events that enliven the grounds such as Indian fairs and competitions of spectacular hoop dancing Overall guests can easily spend two to three hours exploring Take a self guided art walk through Roosevelt Row and the arts district Meghan O Dea Lonely Planet Take part in a self guided art walk on First Fridays Phoenix s First Friday event occurs every first Friday of the month from pm and is one of the nation s largest self guided art walks Guests can experience a variety of artworks at more than galleries event venues and art related spaces across the city â a great way to get to know Phoenix s cultural side Advertisement Planning tip Use the light rail that runs through downtown Phoenix to connect the various art districts The plants at the Desert Botanical Garden in Phoenix are gorgeous colors Mary Elise Photography Shutterstock Desert Botanical Garden runs a free monthly Community Day Community Day at the Desert Botanical Garden allows visitors to enjoy the beauty of the desert for free every second Tuesday of the month reserve tickets in advance The garden is home to thousands of species of cactus trees and flowers from all around the world spread across acres From bluebells to Mexican gold poppies just two of the colorful showstoppers blooming from March to May along the Desert Wildflower Loop Trail this well nurtured botanical garden is a lovely place to reconnect with nature while learning about desert plant life Looping trails lead past a profusion of desert denizens arranged by theme including a Sonoran Desert nature loop and an edible desert garden Planning tip To make the most of your visit plan your route through the property beforehand It s a dazzling spot to check out year round but the flowering spring season is the busiest and most colorful Get hands on at one of the best kids museums in the US The Children s Museum of Phoenix is rated as one of the top three children s museums in the US It s designed for active involvement for children aged up to years old where they can touch explore create and be inspired There are more than play experiences on site spread over three separate floors The museum is open free of charge on at Roosevelt Row Roosevelt Row in the downtown neighborhood is a trendy spot to check out some street art The mission of the area is to advance arts focused initiatives for artists entrepreneurs and residents by cultivating a creative sustainable space for growth opportunity engagement and art advocacy Take a walk and enjoy the dozens of murals and the various locally owned shops bars and city skyline Follow the scenic loop around Tempe Town Lake Walkers cyclists and runners will love the scenic mile loop that encircles Tempe Town Lake A perennial reservoir that was created in Tempe Town Lake serves as a recreational center point for the town of over The path runs on the north and south sides of the lake with bridges connecting at Priest Dr and Mural Rd For those who want to shorten the loop take the pedestrian bridge located near the Tempe Center for the Arts There are numerous pieces of public art lining the lake on both sides of the walkway Take in the cactus views on from the second highest point in the Phoenix Mountains after Camelback Mountain It was named for Arizona local and American Indian soldier Lori Piestewa the first American Indian woman to die serving in the US Armed Forces in Iraq in Planning tip The mile trek to the peak is a tough climb but still hugely popular The park can get extremely busy on winter weekends so plan your parking carefully park is one of the largest municipal parks in the country It s great for hiking mountain biking and horseback riding The mile network leashed dogs allowed dips through canyons over cacti studded hills and past granite walls offering city views and access to ancient petroglyphs Planning tip The main entrance is at S Central Ave Take a hike to South Mountain s Dobbins Lookout which is the highest peak in the park at and enjoy the panoramic view of the city when you make it to the top Go running or biking in Papago Park Known for its massive buttes that rise and fall throughout the park the terrain at Papago Park is primarily made up of sandstone with trails that are considered relatively easy It s a popular area for running and mountain biking Take a leisurely cycle along Tempe Bike
