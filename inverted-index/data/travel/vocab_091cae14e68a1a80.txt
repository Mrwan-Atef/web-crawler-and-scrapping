choice in Athens Login Save The Agora was ancient Athens heart the lively hub of administrative commercial political and social activity Socrates expounded his philosophy here in AD St Paul came here to win converts to Christianity The site today is a lush respite home to the grand Temple of Hephaistos a good museum and the century Byzantine Church of the Holy Apostles trimmed in brick patterns that mimic Arabic calligraphy The greenery harbours birds and lizards Allow about two hours to see everything First developed as a public site in the century BC the Agora was devastated by the Persians in BC but a new one was built in its place almost immediately It was flourishing by Pericles time and continued to do so until AD when it was destroyed by the Herulians a Gothic tribe from Scandinavia The Turks built a residential quarter on the site but this was demolished by archaeologists after Independence and later excavated to Classical and in parts Neolithic levels Admission to
