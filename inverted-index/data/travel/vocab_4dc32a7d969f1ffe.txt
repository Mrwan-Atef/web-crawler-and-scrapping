traditional bakeries selling knafeh a sweet cheese filled pastry Karolina Wiercigroch The city of Nazareth marks the start of the Jesus Trail a mile hiking route that almost literally follows in the footsteps of Christ connecting sites from his life and teachings Taking in dramatic scenery it leads pilgrims along the steep slopes of Mount Arbel and down to the Sea of Galilee Photograph by Karolina Wiercigroch Given its pastoral nature the area is home to a number of organic goat farms For the ultimate Galilean feast tuck into a farm to table meal at Goats with the Wind an off grid farm in a tranquil setting near the ancient settlement of Yodfat Photograph by Karolina Wiercigroch Goats with the Wind is run by Daliah and Amnon Zaldestein who left city life in Haifa for this secluded corner of the Galilee and grew their herd from five to goats They draw on cheesemaking traditions from their travels across France and Italy to produce some unique cheeses which can be enjoyed in hand built gazebos beneath the carob trees Photograph by Karolina Wiercigroch Photograph by Karolina Wiercigroch The Sea of Galilee served as a backdrop to many of Christ s miracles like the Feeding of the which is commemorated by a simple church in Tabgha On the southern shore of the lake between Capernaum and the archaeological site of Ginosar lies the Mount of Beatitudes believed to be where Jesus delivered the Sermon on the Mount The current Church of the Beatitudes was built on the site of Byzantine ruins in for a Franciscan order of nuns and allegedly partly financed by Mussolini The octagonal floor plan represents the eight beatitudes each marked in stained glass just below the dome s golden mosaic The colonnaded cloister balcony and flourishing gardens have spectacular vistas of the northern part of the lake and the cliffs of the Golan Heights Photograph by Karolina Wiercigroch Photograph by Karolina Wiercigroch Combining artisanal initiatives and bountiful local produce the Lower Galilee is a popular destination for gourmet travellers A must visit is Abu Ashraf s memorabilia filled restaurant for katayef sweet thick pancakes folded over nuts and cinnamon then fried with geranium syrup Photograph by Karolina Wiercigroch According to the New Testament Christ famously turned water into wine at a wedding in Cana Indeed the region s wine history dates back thousands of years there s a first century wine press on display in Nazareth Village The thriving wine industry became dormant after the Muslim conquest in the seventh century and the industry didn t return to the area until the century when sweet grape varieties were grown for religious rituals gradually becoming a boutique industry of terroir driven premium wines Karolina Wiercigroch Photograph by Karolina
