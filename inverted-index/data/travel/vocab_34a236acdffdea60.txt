the ocean floor adding a splash of brightness to the murky setting The animal s narrow hard shelled body sports orange green red and blue hues Known as the peacock mantis shrimp this beautiful seven inch long critter looks harmless But the marine animal packs a punch literally By Andrea Silen Common Name Peacock Mantis Shrimp Scientific Name Odontodactylus scyllarus Type Invertebrates Diet Carnivore Size to inches SHRIMP SMACKDOWN Peacock mantis shrimp mostly live in shallow parts of the Indian and Pacific Oceans The crustacean spends much of its time looking for crabs and mollusks to eat When it finds a delicious looking snack the animal goes into full on boxer mode Springing out one of its club like front claws the animal delivers a swift punch to its prey The punch is times faster than the blink of an eye and strong enough to break glass These shrimp also rely on their sparing moves to keep enemies away from burrows in the ocean floor that they use as shelters Hovering at the opening of its burrow a peacock mantis shrimp will strike at intruders that come too close Check out where peacock mantis shrimp live National Geographic Maps Please be respectful of copyright
